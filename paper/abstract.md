# Conference-Style Abstract

Visual-Inertial Odometry (VIO) is a central capability for autonomous aerial robots operating in GPS-denied environments, yet deployed VIO pipelines often degrade silently under motion blur, low texture, illumination changes, sensor inconsistency, and estimator divergence. We present **SHIELD-VIO**, a self-healing research framework for degradation-aware visual-inertial odometry. Instead of treating failure as a terminal tracking-loss event, SHIELD-VIO models estimator reliability as a continuous health state, infers a posterior distribution over degradation causes, and selects online recovery actions. The framework introduces a Navigation Health Index (NHI), an interpretable scalar in `[0, 100]` that fuses perceptual quality, visual residuals, IMU consistency, and uncertainty-related risk. A prototype implementation includes image entropy, motion blur, feature collapse, IMU consistency, and reprojection-error monitors, together with Bayesian failure diagnosis and a rule-based recovery policy. The proposed research agenda evaluates whether health-aware and diagnosis-conditioned VIO can improve failure prediction lead time, tracking uptime, and recovery robustness relative to static VIO configurations. SHIELD-VIO is positioned as a modular layer that can integrate with existing VIO backends and ROS2 autonomy stacks while preserving a clear distinction between implemented prototype components and planned real-time robotic deployment.
